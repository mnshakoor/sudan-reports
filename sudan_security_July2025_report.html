An Intensifying Civil War

As of July 2025, Sudan's civil war is escalating with catastrophic consequences. The conflict between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF) has created the world's largest displacement crisis, fueled by external actors and marked by a deliberate weaponization of aid. The Anatomy of the Conflict

This section explores the core dynamics of the civil war: the primary factions locked in a struggle for power, the shifting tides of battle across the country, and the complex web of external actors fueling the violence. Understanding these elements is key to grasping the conflict's resilience and the challenges to peace.

Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF)

Leader: Gen. Abdel Fattah al-Burhan

Objective: Regain full state control and establish a unified, technocratic government.

Key Allies & Support:

  • Reportedly supported by Iran.
  • Received offers of military aid from Russia, shifting from prior RSF support.
  • Supported by Ukraine's special forces against Russian mercenaries.
  • Allied with internal militias like the Sudan Shield Forces (post-defection) and the al-Bara Ibn Malik Brigade.

Rapid Support Forces (RSF)

Leader: Gen. Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo (Hemedti)

Objective: Solidify authority in controlled areas, potentially through a parallel government structure, and disrupt SAF logistics.

Key Allies & Support:

  • Extensive alleged support from the United Arab Emirates (UAE), including weapons and drones.
  • Historical support from Russia's Wagner Group, primarily for gold concessions.
  • Manages strategic relationships with Chad due to control of the border region.
  • Alleged weapon supplies from Kenya, though denied.

A Catastrophic Human Toll

The war has triggered the world's largest displacement crisis and a humanitarian catastrophe of unprecedented scale. Famine, a collapsed healthcare system, and rampant disease outbreaks define the daily reality for millions of Sudanese civilians, with aid organizations facing severe access constraints and funding shortfalls.

Food Insecurity & Famine

Famine is confirmed in multiple areas, driven by conflict, high food prices, and the weaponization of aid. Both sides block or restrict relief, leading to one of history's most severe food crises.

Health System Collapse

70% of medical facilities are non-operational. Attacks on hospitals are frequent. Outbreaks of cholera, malaria, and measles are rampant, with malnutrition rates among the world's highest.

Aid Access Constraints

Humanitarian access has severely deteriorated. Aid workers are threatened and attacked, infrastructure is damaged, and both SAF and RSF impose bureaucratic and physical blockades on lifesaving relief.

A Fragmented International Response

Despite the escalating crisis, diplomatic efforts to broker peace have been largely unsuccessful. Mediation tracks are fragmented, and the conflicting interests of external powers undermine a unified international strategy, while warring parties consistently fail to adhere to ceasefire agreements.

Jeddah Process

Brokered by Saudi Arabia and the U.S., this key diplomatic track has failed to achieve meaningful breakthroughs as hostilities persist. The focus on civilian protection remains largely unimplemented.

African Union (AU) & IGAD

The AU and IGAD advocate for a unified, Sudanese-led peace process. They host consultative meetings to coordinate efforts but struggle to enforce compliance or overcome external interference.

United Nations (UN)

The UN monitors human rights abuses, coordinates the humanitarian response, and calls for ceasefires. However, its efforts are hampered by violence, aid blockades, and divisions within the Security Council.
